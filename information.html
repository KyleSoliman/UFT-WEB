About the Game

Persona 5 is the sixth installment in the Persona series, initially released for PlayStation 3 and PlayStation 4 in Japan on September 15, 2016.

The game takes place in modern-day Tokyo, starring Joker, the codename of a high school student who transfers to a new school while he is on probation after being framed for assault.

Persona 5 (including Royal) sold over 7.2 million copies by April 2023, making it the best-selling title in the Megami Tensei franchise.

SYSTEM 01
Plot

The story is told through flashbacks during Sae Niijima's interrogation of the protagonist. After being framed for assault, the protagonist is put on probation and sent to Tokyo to stay with family friend Sojiro Sakura while attending Shujin Academy.

Upon arrival, he enters the Velvet Room where Igor and his assistants Caroline and Justine warn him of future ruin and grant him access to a supernatural app. This leads him into the Metaverse and the Palace of corrupt gym teacher Suguru Kamoshida, alongside fellow student Ryuji Sakamoto.

After being captured by Kamoshida's Shadow Self, the protagonist awakens his Persona Arsène to save Ryuji. They escape and meet Morgana, a cat-like creature who explains how to change wicked hearts by stealing their "Treasure" from Palaces. Learning of Kamoshida's abuse, Ryuji also awakens his Persona.

The group confronts Ann Takamaki, who was forced into a relationship with Kamoshida to protect her friend Shiho Suzui. When Shiho attempts suicide after being assaulted, the group decides to steal Kamoshida's heart. Ann awakens her Persona after accidentally entering the Metaverse.

The protagonist takes the codename Joker and forms the Phantom Thieves of Hearts with Morgana, Ryuji, and Ann. They recruit additional members: Yusuke Kitagawa (artist), Makoto Niijima (student council president), Futaba Sakura (hacker whose mother was murdered by the conspiracy), and Haru Okumura (corporate heiress rebelling against her father).

The Phantom Thieves' popularity plummets after a masked assassin frames them for Haru's father's death. Goro Akechi joins the group and convinces them to change Sae's heart. After infiltrating Sae's Palace, Joker is captured and interrogated, bringing the story full-circle.

Akechi is revealed as the assassin and attempts to kill Joker, but the Phantom Thieves fake his death using the Metaverse. They discover the conspiracy's leader is politician Masayoshi Shido, who framed Joker and has been using Akechi's abilities to eliminate obstacles on his path to becoming Prime Minister.

During Shido's Palace infiltration, Akechi reveals himself as Shido's illegitimate son seeking revenge. After being defeated, Akechi sacrifices himself to protect the Phantom Thieves. Despite Shido's arrest, public opinion remains unchanged due to manipulation by his allies.

The Phantom Thieves enter Mementos' depths to change the public's hearts but discover people have chosen to give up their autonomy. They're ejected by the Holy Grail as the Metaverse merges with reality, causing them to vanish.

In the Velvet Room, the false Igor is revealed to be Yaldabaoth, the God of Control created from humanity's wish to be free from suffering. Caroline and Justine regain their memories and become Lavenza, revealing Yaldabaoth imprisoned the real Igor and manipulated both Joker and Akechi through a wager about humanity's goals.

Yaldabaoth offers to restore the world at the cost of freedom. If Joker accepts, an alternate ending occurs where the Phantom Thieves gain fame but humanity loses free will. If rejected, Joker reunites with his team, and with his confidants' support, the people rebel against Yaldabaoth's control.

Joker awakens his ultimate Persona, Satanael, to destroy Yaldabaoth and the Metaverse. After the victory, Joker turns himself in so Shido can be prosecuted. While incarcerated, his friends gather evidence proving his innocence, leading to his conviction being overturned. By spring, his friends drive him back to his hometown.

(Excluding the plot of Royal)

SYSTEM 02
Development

2008-2010

  • July 2008 - Persona 5 starts conceptual development, after Persona 4 is released in Japan.
  • July 2, 2009 - Persona 5 is accidentally leaked on pachinko gaming website 7-Rush before it was quickly removed.
  • March 24, 2010 - Hashino confirms in Dengeki PlayStation that the next numbered title of the Persona series is in development, saying that he wants to add in stuff "that fans wouldn't expect".

2011

  • February 2 - "Preliminary development" begins on the next Persona entry after the release of Catherine, using a new engine rather than GameByro.
  • March 11 - The initial idea gets completely reworked due to the 2011 Tohoku Earthquake.
  • August 31 - Preliminary development of the new Persona game "nears completion" in the August issue of Famitsu.

2012

  • August 8 - Hashino says in Famitsu that the developmental team is focused on creating the new entry in the series.

2013

  • June 25 - The domain name for the website "persona5.jp" is registered by Atlus.
  • September 26 - A countdown on p-ch.jp was posted, where it said that "a new title" would be announced.
  • November 24 - Persona 5 is officially revealed by Atlus, where the game is confirmed to be coming to the PlayStation 3 in Winter 2014.

2014

  • February 24 - Atlus confirms that Persona 5 is coming to the west, with an English release date of "2015".
  • September - A teaser trailer was posted, where the game was confirmed to be coming to the PlayStation 4, while its release date was moved to "2015".

2015

  • Feb. 5 - PV 01 trailer was released, showing off the gameplay.
  • June 24 - At E3 2015, Persona 5 was confirmed to still be coming in 2015 in English.
  • September 17-19 - Persona 5 was shown off at Tokyo Game Show 2015, revealing that the game was delayed to "Summer 2016". A new trailer was released.
  • December 5 - Date of the December build.

2016

  • February 25 - Build date of the debug prototype.
  • April 22 - Atlus announces a countdown on April 22, where it would end with a livestream on May 5, 2016.
  • May - Persona 5 finishes development.
  • September 15 - Persona 5 is officially released in Japan.

2017

  • April 4 - Persona 5 is officially released in America and Europe.
